SCU Staff

Taylor L. Dempsey


Silver City Uprising Chairwoman

Organized, Creative, Vibrant Worker



I am a born and raised native of Newington, CT. My genetic makeup is of Irish, African American and Native American decent. I graduated from Newington High School and later went on to teach there. I never knew that I would teach at the very school where I experienced so much joy. I was saddened when my teaching experience was overshadowed by racism, the exact opposite of my expectations, first hand. We all know the American dark secret exists but when you’re on the receiving end, it leaves a scar forever. I have made it my business to combat racial intolerance through activism. I am a leader in the Black Live Matter movement. I have brought many people together of all races. I co-founded the Ally Collective and CT Peace Festival. I was excited when the team at Silver City Uprising asked me to apply for the chair position. I graduated from Central University, Summa Cum Laude. I received the Highest Achievement award in Foreign Language, Latin. My honor societies include: The English Honor Society, Sigma Tau Delta, Professional Honor Society, and Alpha Lambda Delta. In light of all those achievements there is nothing like being on the front lines for the economic empowerment of the black community. I stand strong in those beliefs and refuse to be silenced. Every black life matter and until those words are unuttered in the police department, schools and legislation takes action on our behalf, I will always be on that battlefield. I valued working as a student worker for Veteran Affairs Helping student veterans, assisting with certifications for enrollment, helping with homework or other responsibilities. As student worker my most exciting experience was with the Veterans History Project where I Interviewed veterans in their homes, collecting/organizing photographs or pertinent documents, uploading and maintaining website profiles of veterans, answering phones, working with faculty and various personnel, working with digital tools, digitally formatting projects, organized historical trip to Gettysburg, PA. I now look forward to contributing to the success of Silver City Uprising.




Patrina Medina

Director of Fundraising



resides in Meriden, CT and I have been married for 27 years with 2 children ages 19 and 14. A Georgia native, moved to Meriden when she was 2 years old. I was educated by the Meriden public schools and graduated from Platt High School in 1990. I received my Bachelors of Science for Human Services in 2016 from Springfield College. I plan on pursuing my Master's Degree in the near future. I got married in 1993 to my High School sweetheart & he also received his education from the Meriden public school system. My love for children has led me from working at a daycare to working at my current job, which is The Department of Children's and Families, for over 13 years. Growing up in Meriden and deciding to become a homeowner at age 23 in the same town I grew up in and to raise my family here had a lot to do with my relationship with my own family and the love and potential I saw in my city. My family is a very close-knit family and we see each other often at family events and holidays. My parents and my 4 siblings still reside in Meriden, CT. I have seen Meriden transform over the years into what it is today. We now have a Meriden Green, where things for the community can take place. I like to take walks at the Meriden Linear Trail & The Quinnipiac Trail of Meriden. I also like to visit Hubbard Park and Castle Craig at times. I love talking with people in the community. I help my church with providing donations to the Salvation Army and the homeless shelter during the winter. I love helping people in any way I can. In doing that and in my line of work I see a need to help the underprivileged community and minorities that live in this town. Resources don't seem to be readily available to them. Knowledge is power and I am all about the empowerment of the people and the community that we live A activities for our youth to participate in, at a fair cost to the people in the community seem hard to find. We don't want their attention to be drawn to other things. We want to help the people in our community become productive members of society to succeed at whatever it is they would like to do in life. I think Silver City Uprising encompasses this and much more! This is why I chose to be a part of this organization.




Debbie D. Sears

Board Treasurer



Debbie has over 20 years of healthcare experience as she started her career at Aetna as a work study student from Maloney High School. She held numerous positions throughout her tenure with Aetna of 25 years. Her first assignment was an office assistant for the fraud department, Cash Application Specialist, Escheat Analyst, Disability Team Leader and Senior Claims Reporting Analyst. She currently holds the position of a Pharmacy Benefit Representative for Connecticare Inc. and has been employed with the insurance company for 6 years. After she decided to take an early retirement from Aetna, she embarked on a new career. She studied Pharmacy Technology at Middlesex Community College in Middletown, CT. She is a licensed Pharmacy Technician. At an early age caring for her grandmother she saw the need to educate the elderly and community on prescribed prescription drugs. She is very successful in her current role. In her personal life she adores her son Damian who will be a senior this year in college. She is very active in her church, Mount Hebron Baptist Church in Meriden, CT. The Lord is first in her life. She is on auxiliaries such as the Deaconess ministry, Trustee ministry and served as the financial secretary/treasurer for 10 years. She is on the Scholarship Committee for the past 8 years. Debbie also served as the property manager for their rental properties. Most recently in the passing of her mother, she took over the Angel Tree ministry as the Chairperson. She is now in her third successful year. Fun fact about Debbie is she loves music and shoes. She wrote three songs and paid for them with her allowance at the age of 14, she saw an ad in the Word up magazine. She officially owns the rights to three songs. Her current goal is to own her own business.




Brigitta Rainey

Board Secretary



Graduated Magnum Cum Laude, Springfield College 2017 Brigitta has been a CT State Employee for the last 31 years and is currently an Equal Employment Opportunity Assistant, Affirmative Action. Brigitta is a BLM activist and firm believer of Human Rights. She was featured in the Meriden Record Journal in 1995 in her brave challenge to then U.S Representative Gary Franks, to a debate on Affirmative Action, a program for which Mr. Franks campaigned to abolish. Brigitta was recognized by the Hartford Courant in April 1996 for sponsoring an Easter Basket drive for 23 children living at Saint Vincent DePaul homeless shelter in Meriden. As a survivor of domestic abuse, Brigitta shares a compassion for helping people and values being a part of Silver City Uprising’s mission as a founder and organizer. Her motto is "Don't count the days, make the days count"! https://meritpages.com/brigittalove




Ty Rainey
